# Marketing Leaders' Core Needs & Output Goals

This document outlines the core needs and objectives for each marketing persona. The scenario builder's output should be tailored to directly address these specific challenges, providing tangible value beyond generic feature lists.

## Retailer: "Make it Real, Make it Easy"

The primary goal for a retail specialist is to empower floor staff to close sales by making complex technology feel simple, relevant, and immediately valuable to the customer standing in front of them. The generated scenario must directly address their real-world challenges.

### Core Challenges & Output Solutions:

1.  **Challenge: Overcoming Customer Indifference ("So what?").**
    *   **Need:** Translate abstract features into tangible personal benefits (e.g., time saved, money saved, convenience).
    *   **Output Goal:** The scenario must explicitly script how a feature (e.g., AI Energy Mode) leads to a real-world outcome ("...this saves you up to 15% on your electricity bill, which is like getting a free coffee every week.").

2.  **Challenge: Breaking Down the Installation Barrier ("It looks too complicated").**
    *   **Need:** De-risk the purchase by making the initial setup experience feel foolproof.
    *   **Output Goal:** Provide a simple, step-by-step "Quick Start Guide" within the scenario (e.g., "Just scan this QR code with the SmartThings app, and it's a simple 3-step process."). This directly addresses the user's fear of a frustrating first-use experience.

3.  **Challenge: Creating Opportunities for Upselling ("I only came for one thing").**
    *   **Need:** Naturally introduce other products in the ecosystem without aggressive sales pressure.
    *   **Output Goal:** The scenario should include "Ecosystem Expansion" talking points. For example, after demonstrating a TV scene, the script should suggest: "And imagine how much more immersive this would be if the lights dimmed automatically. That's what happens when you add our smart lights to the experience."

4.  **Challenge: Ensuring Post-Sale Satisfaction ("What if it breaks?").**
    *   **Need:** Reduce the fear of post-purchase issues and build long-term trust.
    *   **Output Goal:** Include "Self-Care & Troubleshooting" tips in the scenario. This empowers the customer to solve minor issues on their own using the SmartThings app's diagnostic tools, positioning Samsung as a brand that offers continuous support.

## Dotcom: "Guide the Journey, Prove the ROI"

The Dotcom marketer's world revolves around data-driven optimization of the online customer journey. Their goal is to convert anonymous traffic into loyal, app-using customers. The scenario must provide them with concrete, measurable strategies and content to deploy across the digital funnel.

### Core Challenges & Output Solutions:

1.  **Challenge: High Bounce Rates & Low Conversion ("Why don't they click 'Buy'?").**
    *   **Need:** Move beyond feature lists and sell the "experience" on the Product Detail Page (PDP).
    *   **Output Goal:** Generate "Experience-Led" PDP content, including A/B testable headlines and value propositions that frame the product's benefits in the context of the customer's life. Provide clear Cause-and-Effect metrics (e.g., "Applying this content is expected to increase 'Add to Cart' clicks by 5%").

2.  **Challenge: Overcoming App Installation Friction ("Why bother with another app?").**
    *   **Need:** Capitalize on the "golden window" of post-purchase excitement to drive app adoption.
    *   **Output Goal:** Create a "gamified" onboarding mission to be included in purchase confirmation pages and emails. This frames the app setup not as a chore, but as the exciting first step into a smarter life, directly addressing the "friction of first use."

    *   **Need:** Nurture customers post-purchase with personalized, relevant communication that encourages repeat use and ecosystem expansion.
    *   **Output Goal:** Provide automated marketing scenarios based on customer data. For example: "For customers who have activated 'AI Energy Mode', send a push notification after 2 weeks showing their actual savings, then suggest another energy-saving routine." This provides a clear reason for repeat engagement and introduces the path to ecosystem expansion.

## Brand: "Build the Love, Tell the Story"

The Brand Marketer is the guardian of the brand's soul. They focus on long-term perception and emotional connection rather than immediate sales. Their goal is to build a "lovable" brand by telling compelling stories and crafting a consistent, authentic brand personality. The scenario must provide them with the core narrative and creative assets to build this emotional bridge.

### Core Challenges & Output Solutions:

1.  **Challenge: Making Technology Feel Human ("AI feels cold and robotic").**
    *   **Need:** Define a clear and warm brand philosophy for complex technologies like AI.
    *   **Output Goal:** Provide a "Brand Narrative & Core Philosophy" section. This should define the brand's unique take on technology (e.g., "Thoughtful Intelligence") and offer core messages that translate this into a warm, human-centric story, distinguishing the brand from the cold, functional positioning of competitors.

2.  **Challenge: Creating an Emotional Connection ("Features don't build fans").**
    *   **Need:** Move beyond product features to create relatable, "shareable" moments that resonate with customers' lives.
    *   **Output Goal:** Generate a "Social Video Campaign Storyboard." This should not be a product demo, but a short, emotional narrative showing how the technology seamlessly and beautifully fits into a real-life moment (e.g., a child's birthday party, a quiet evening). This gives the brand marketer a ready-to-use creative concept.

3.  **Challenge: Differentiating the Brand Experience ("How are we different from Apple?").**
    *   **Need:** Articulate a unique, tangible brand experience that competitors cannot easily replicate.
    *   **Output Goal:** Propose an "Offline Experience Concept" (e.g., for a pop-up store). The concept should be an interactive installation that brings the brand's core philosophy to life. For Samsung, this means highlighting "open connectivity" and "personalization" in a way that contrasts with the "closed ecosystem" of competitors, making the brand's unique value proposition tangible.
